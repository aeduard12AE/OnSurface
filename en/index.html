About the project

A story-driven niche RPG built around three characters standing at turning points in their lives. Their past is only a brief sketch; their future is shaped by the choices that are made.

Those choices affect not only the protagonists, but also the world around them—often quietly, and not at once. The fate of the world does not rest on you; no grand feats await—only decisions made with an incomplete grasp of their consequences.

And perhaps, through these private stories, there is a rare chance to draw closer to an answer to a question that has unsettled inquisitive minds for centuries: why the world is the way it is.

Developer log

Devlog #0

Self-introduction

I’ve been playing games since childhood, and I’ve always gravitated toward single-player RPGs with stories. Over time there were fewer people around and more solitude, and at some point games stopped being entertainment and became a form of dialogue.

This project is an attempt to create something that could feel like an extension of myself. It grew out of my thoughts, oddities, and a persistent need to understand human motives and the way social perception takes shape.

I’m not interested in winning or proving anything to the world—I’m interested in observing. Including how, through decisions inside this story, someone else’s understanding of my view of the world does—or does not—appear.

This log is simply a way to record the path and the thoughts along the way. No plan, no obligations, no certainty about the outcome.

Devlog #2

The road ahead

By the time the demo is released, the game reaches a point where it can be shared. This is the first chapter—an opening rather than a finished form, the moment when the world stops being closed and begins to exist beyond my notes and reflections.

In the near future, it matters to me to see how the demo is experienced: what choices are made, where doubt appears, which moments resonate, and which pass unnoticed. I’m less interested in scores and more in the experience itself.

Right now I’m considering a few possible paths forward. One is to keep working until the game is fully complete and release all chapters at once, when the story is finished. Another is to open the project earlier and release it in parts: after Chapter Two, move into Early Access and gradually build the game toward its final chapter.

Both options have their limits and their strengths, and the final decision will largely depend on how the game is received at this stage. That’s why any feedback—thoughts, doubts, noticed inconsistencies, or simply the feeling you’re left with after playing— is especially valuable to me right now.

If you’d like to share anything, you can do so via the feedback form on the site. I read every message, even if I can’t always respond right away.
